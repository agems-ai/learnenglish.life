# 10 TOEFL Speaking Tips to Boost Your Score

The Speaking section is often the most nerve-wracking part of the TOEFL. Use these ten tips to stay calm and score high.

1.  **Use Templates:** Have a structure ready for each task (e.g., "In my opinion...", "First...", "Second...").
2.  **Monitor Your Pace:** Don't speak too fast. Clarity is more important than speed.
3.  **Take Effective Notes:** Use symbols and abbreviations to quickly jot down main ideas from the reading and listening passages.
4.  **Focus on Intonation:** Avoid speaking in a monotone. Use natural English stress and rhythm.
5.  **Don't Worry About Your Accent:** As long as you are clear and understandable, a minor accent won't hurt your score.
6.  **Practice with a Timer:** The Speaking section has very strict time limits.
7.  **Use Transitional Words:** Words like "However," "Therefore," and "In addition" show a high level of organization.
8.  **Elaborate on Your Points:** Don't just give a one-sentence answer. Explain *why* you feel a certain way.
9.  **Listen Carefully to the Prompt:** Make sure you are answering the specific question asked.
10. **Record Yourself:** Listen to your own practice responses to identify where you stumble or repeat yourself.

## Conclusion

With consistent practice and a clear structure, you can master the Speaking section. Focus on being clear, organized, and confident.
