# TOEFL vs IELTS: Which Exam Should You Take in 2026?

Choosing between the TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) and the IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is a major decision for any student or professional looking to move abroad. Both are globally recognized, but they offer very different experiences.

## Key Differences at a Glance

| Feature | TOEFL (iBT) | IELTS (Academic) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Format** | 100% Computer-based | Paper or Computer-based |
| **Speaking** | Recorded into a microphone | Face-to-face interview |
| **Listening** | Academic lectures (US/UK accents) | Variety of accents and contexts |
| **Duration** | ~2 hours | ~2 hours 45 minutes |

## Which One is Right for You?

### Choose TOEFL if:
* You are comfortable speaking to a computer.
* You prefer multiple-choice questions.
* You are applying primarily to US-based universities.

### Choose IELTS if:
* You prefer a more "human" interaction for the speaking test.
* You like a variety of question types (matching, labeling, etc.).
* You are applying to schools in the UK, Australia, or Canada.

## Conclusion

Both exams are fair assessments of your English ability. The best way to decide is to take a practice test for each and see which format feels more natural to you.
