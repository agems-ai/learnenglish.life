What is Duolingo?

Duolingo is a free language learning app that makes studying English fun. More than 500 million people have downloaded it worldwide. The app uses games, short lessons, and daily challenges to keep you motivated.

You can use Duolingo on your phone, tablet, or computer. Lessons take only 5-10 minutes each. This makes it easy to practice every day, even if you are busy. The app tracks your "streak" — how many days in a row you have studied. Many users love this feature because it creates a habit.

Duolingo was founded in 2011 and is based in Pittsburgh, USA. It offers courses in 40+ languages, including English for speakers of Spanish, Chinese, French, and many other languages.

How Does It Work?

Duolingo organizes learning into "skills." Each skill has several lessons. You earn points (called "Lingots" or "Gems") for completing lessons. You lose points if you answer incorrectly. This gamification keeps users engaged and coming back.

Lessons include:

  • Translation exercises — Translate sentences between English and your language
  • Listening practice — Hear sentences and choose the correct answer
  • Speaking drills — Record yourself speaking English and get feedback
  • Fill-in-the-blank — Complete sentences with missing words
  • Matching games — Connect words with their meanings

The app uses "spaced repetition" — it shows you words again just before you forget them. This helps you remember vocabulary better. You also unlock new levels as you progress, which keeps the learning feeling fresh.

Final Verdict

Duolingo is an excellent starting point for learning English. The free access, gamified lessons, and bite-sized format make it perfect for beginners. If you have never studied English before, Duolingo will teach you basic vocabulary and simple sentences.

However, Duolingo alone will not make you fluent. The grammar explanations are shallow, and you cannot practice real conversations with the app. Once you finish the basics, you will need to add other tools — like a tutor on iTalki or structured lessons — to reach intermediate or advanced levels.

Our recommendation: Start with Duolingo to build a daily habit and learn vocabulary. After 2-3 months, add a tutoring platform like iTalki for speaking practice. Together, these tools create a strong foundation for English fluency.
