What is Busuu?

Busuu is a language learning platform founded in 2008 that combines self-paced lessons with community features. Unlike purely app-based learning, Busuu offers exercises reviewed by native speakers and structured courses aligned with CEFR language standards.

The platform focuses on practical communication skills with courses designed by linguistics experts. Each unit covers vocabulary, grammar, dialogues, and cultural notes. The adaptive algorithm personalizes your learning path based on your performance.

Busuu's unique differentiator is the community feature. You can submit exercises to native speakers for feedback, and you help other learners in return. This creates a reciprocal learning environment.

Key Features

1. Structured Courses

Busuu offers comprehensive courses aligned with CEFR levels (A1-C2). Each unit includes vocabulary, grammar, listening, speaking, and writing exercises with clear learning objectives.

2. Native Speaker Corrections

Premium users can submit writing exercises to native speakers for feedback. In return, you correct exercises for other learners. Creating a helpful community.

3. Personalized Learning Path

The algorithm adapts to your level and learning pace. It identifies gaps in your knowledge and creates a customized study plan.

4. Cultural Insights

Each lesson includes cultural notes about English-speaking countries, helping you understand context beyond grammar and vocabulary.

5. Official Certificates

Complete a full course and receive a certificate. These are recognized by McGraw-Hill Education and can be shared on LinkedIn.

Our Verdict

Busuu is an excellent middle-ground between free apps and formal tutoring. It offers more structure than Duolingo while remaining more affordable than platforms like iTalki.

The native speaker corrections are valuable for improving writing, and the structured curriculum helps learners progress systematically. However, there's no speaking practice with real people.

Busuu is best for:

  • Intermediate learners wanting structure
  • People who prefer quality over gamification
  • Those who want certificates for their resume
  • Learners focused on grammar and writing
