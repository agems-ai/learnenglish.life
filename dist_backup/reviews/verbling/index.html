What Is Verbling?

Verbling is an online language tutoring marketplace that connects learners with professional language teachers for 1-on-1 video lessons. Founded in 2011 by language enthusiasts at the University of California, Berkeley, Verbling has built a reputation as a premium marketplace where quality tutors and motivated learners come together.

What sets Verbling apart is its community-first approach. The platform hosts live events, study groups, and language exchanges between learners studying the same language. It also emphasizes teacher quality: Verbling actively promotes tutors with formal teaching credentials and documented results.

How Does Verbling Work?

Getting started on Verbling follows a familiar marketplace pattern:

  1. Create your profile. Tell Verbling what language you want to learn, your current level, and your goals.
  2. Browse and filter tutors. Search by language, price range, availability, teaching style, and certifications.
  3. Book a trial lesson. Almost every tutor on Verbling offers a discounted trial session so you can assess the fit.
  4. Schedule your lessons. Use the booking system to schedule sessions that fit your calendar. Many tutors offer flexible scheduling, including evenings and weekends.
  5. Access the Verbling community. Join study groups, attend live events, and connect with other learners on the platform.

Pricing

Verbling operates on a pay-per-lesson model similar to Preply. However, the average tutor prices on Verbling tend to run higher than on other marketplaces. This reflects the platform's positioning toward professional, quality-focused tutors.

The average hourly rate for a quality tutor on Verbling is around $25 to $35. Trial lessons are typically priced at $5 to $10. Verbling also offers a small discount when you purchase lesson bundles, though the discount is generally less generous than what you find on some competing platforms.

Teacher Quality

Verbling has cultivated a reputation for attracting higher-quality tutors than average. The platform makes it easy to identify well-qualified teachers through its certification badges, detailed profiles, and student reviews. You will find a higher concentration of CELTA and DELTA certified teachers on Verbling compared to most other marketplaces.

That said, quality still varies. Here is how to find the best tutors on Verbling:

  • Look for certification badges. CELTA, DELTA, TEFL, and TESOL certifications are displayed prominently on tutor profiles.
  • Read recent reviews. Focus on feedback from the past six months. This gives you the most accurate picture of current teaching quality.
  • Check response times. Tutors with fast response times are generally more engaged and reliable.
  • Review their teaching methodology. Many tutors describe their approach on their profile. Look for methods that align with your learning style.
  • Book the trial first. Always start with a trial session. The investment of a few dollars saves you from committing to a poorly matched tutor.

The Marketplace Model

Like Preply and iTalki, Verbling is a marketplace, which means you have full control over who you learn from. You are not assigned a teacher; you choose them. This is excellent for learners who know what they want, but it can be overwhelming for those who are new to online tutoring.

One advantage Verbling has over some competitors is the FlixTutorial feature: some tutors offer structured mini-courses with pre-designed lesson plans. This adds a curriculum layer that most marketplaces lack, giving learners who want more structure an option within the platform.

Scheduling and Flexibility

Verbling's scheduling system is intuitive and integrates with your calendar. Most tutors on the platform have flexible availability, including evenings and weekends, which makes it easier for working professionals to find workable lesson times.

However, because you are booking with individual tutors rather than a platform that manages teacher availability, you may encounter scheduling challenges when you want to increase your lesson frequency. If your preferred tutor is unavailable, you either wait or find someone else.

Cancellation policies are set by individual tutors, so they vary. Most offer full refunds for cancellations made 24 hours in advance.

Unique Features

  • Verbling community. The platform includes a community of learners studying the same languages. You can participate in group discussions, language exchanges, and study groups.
  • Live events. Verbling hosts regular live events, workshops, and webinars led by tutors and language experts.
  • Flix Tutorials. Some tutors offer structured video-based mini-courses that supplement your 1-on-1 lessons.
  • Smart Calendar. Verbling's scheduling tool syncs with Google Calendar and Apple Calendar for seamless booking.
  • Video classroom. Lessons take place in Verbling's browser-based video room with shared whiteboard and text tools.
  • Mobile app. Manage lessons and communicate with tutors on the iOS or Android app.

Verdict

Verbling is a solid choice for learners who are serious about progressing and are willing to pay a bit more for quality. The platform attracts professional, well-qualified teachers, and the community features add genuine value that you do not get on other marketplaces.

If you are an intermediate-to-advanced learner who knows what you want from your English lessons and you want a tutor with real credentials, Verbling is worth exploring. The Flix Tutorial feature is a nice bonus for learners who want structure alongside their conversation practice.

However, if budget is your primary concern, you will find more affordable options on larger marketplaces. And if you are a complete beginner who needs significant guidance, the lack of a standardized curriculum means you still need to take an active role in directing your learning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Verbling more expensive than iTalki or Preply?

On average, yes. Verbling tends to attract more certified, professional teachers who charge premium rates. However, you can still find affordable tutors on Verbling, particularly newer teachers building their reputation. The platform covers a wide price range.

Can I learn multiple languages on Verbling?

Yes. Verbling supports lessons in many languages, not just English. You can take Spanish, French, German, Japanese, and dozens of other languages on the same subscription if you have tutors booked for each.

How is Verbling different from Preply?

Both are marketplaces with similar models. Verbling tends to attract more certified, professional teachers and includes community features like live events and study groups. Preply has a larger tutor base and more price variety. The right choice depends on your budget and how much you value teacher credentials.

Does Verbling have a mobile app?

Yes, Verbling has mobile apps for iOS and Android. The apps let you manage your lessons, message tutors, and access your learning materials on the go.

What are Flix Tutorials?

Flix Tutorials are structured video-based mini-courses created by some Verbling tutors. They provide a curriculum that supplements your 1-on-1 lessons, which is useful for learners who want more guided progression than conversation practice alone offers.
