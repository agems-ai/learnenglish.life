Best English Learning Apps for Kids: Top 5 Apps Compared

Fun, safe, and effective apps to help your child learn English from ages 2 to 12.

Quick Verdict

Best overall: Lingokids. Ages 2-8, gamified learning, affordable

Best for older kids: Duolingo Kids. Ages 6-12, structured curriculum

Best free option: Khan Academy Kids. All ages, completely free

Children learn languages naturally when they're having fun. The right app can make English learning feel like play — not homework. But not all apps are created equal. Some are just games with English words. Others teach real skills.

We tested the most popular kids' English learning apps. Here's what works.

How We Tested

  • Educational value. does it teach real English?
  • Child engagement. do kids want to keep using it?
  • Safety. no ads, no in-app purchases for kids
  • Parent features. progress tracking, time limits
  • Value for money. worth the subscription?

Top 5 English Learning Apps for Kids

1. Lingokids. Best Overall

★★★★★ 8.5/10

Age range: 2-8 years old

Price: Free version available; Lingokids World: $79.88/year (about $6.66/month)

Lingokids uses games, songs, and videos to teach English. The colorful interface keeps young children engaged. Over 5,000 activities cover vocabulary, listening, reading, and speaking.

What we like:

  • Gamified learning with rewards and characters
  • No ads in the premium version
  • Progress tracking for parents
  • Covers all four skills: reading, writing, listening, speaking
  • Playlist feature for parents to guide learning

What could be better: Limited value for kids over age 8. Some activities feel more like games than learning.

2. Duolingo Kids. Best for Structured Learning

★★★★☆ 8.7/10

Age range: 6-12 years old

Price: Included with Duolingo Plus ($12.99/month) or standalone Duolingo Kids $6.99/month

Duolingo Kids brings the popular Duolingo method to children. Short lessons (2-5 minutes), bright colors, and gamification keep kids motivated. The curriculum follows a clear progression from basics to intermediate skills.

What we like:

  • Clear learning progression with levels
  • Fun characters and streak rewards
  • Covers vocabulary, grammar, and listening
  • Easy for children to use independently
  • Based on Duolingo's proven methodology

What could be better: Limited speaking practice in the app. Can feel repetitive after a while. Speaking features require kids to read instructions.

3. Khan Academy Kids. Best Free Option

★★★★☆ 8.3/10

Age range: 2-8 years old

Price: Completely free

Khan Academy Kids offers thousands of activities covering reading, math, logic, and more. While not exclusively an English learning app, the reading activities are excellent for building literacy skills.

What we like:

  • 100% free. no subscriptions or ads
  • Offline mode available
  • Excellent reading and phonics content
  • Progress tracking for parents
  • Created by education experts

What could be better: Not focused specifically on English as a second language. Less gamified than other options.

4. ABCmouse. Best for Comprehensive Learning

★★★★☆ 8.4/10

Age range: 2-8 years old

Price: $12.99/month or $79.99/year

ABCmouse offers a full learning path with over 10,000 activities. The step-by-step curriculum covers reading, math, science, and art. The "Learning Path" feature guides children through activities in order.

What we like:

  • Comprehensive curriculum covering many subjects
  • Learning Path keeps children on track
  • Huge variety of activities
  • Offline mode available
  • Excellent for building foundational skills

What could be better: More expensive than alternatives. Can feel overwhelming with too many activities.

5. Endless Wordplay. Best for Vocabulary

★★★★☆ 8.1/10

Age range: 3-7 years old

Price: $7.99/month or $59.99 one-time purchase

Endless Wordplay focuses specifically on vocabulary and spelling. The animated monsters make learning word patterns fun. Each level teaches a group of related words (like "-ight" words: light, night, fight).

What we like:

  • Excellent for vocabulary building
  • Fun animations and characters
  • Teaches word patterns, not just memorization
  • No ads or in-app purchases

What could be better: Narrow focus. only vocabulary, not full language skills. Best used alongside another app.

Tips for Parents

Apps are helpful, but they're not a replacement for real interaction. Here's how to get the most from English learning apps:

Do:

  • Use apps together with your child initially to guide them
  • Set time limits (30-45 minutes per day max)
  • Check progress reports weekly
  • Praise effort, not just completion
  • Combine app learning with real English conversation

Don't:

  • Let children use apps unsupervised for long periods
  • Use apps as a babysitter. active learning needs parental involvement
  • Focus only on screen time. reading books together is equally important

Final Verdict

For most families, we recommend starting with Duolingo Kids or Lingokids. Both are engaging, educational, and worth the subscription cost.

If budget is a concern, Khan Academy Kids is an excellent free option. It's not specifically designed for ESL learners, but the reading content is high quality.

Remember: apps are a tool, not a complete solution. The best results come from combining app learning with real conversations, reading books together, and making English part of your child's daily life.
