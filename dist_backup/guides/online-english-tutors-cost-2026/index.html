One of the first questions people ask when starting to learn English online is: how much will it cost? The answer depends on many factors, including which platform you choose, the experience level of your tutor, and how often you want to take lessons. This guide breaks down everything you need to know about online English tutor prices in 2026.

What affects the cost of online English lessons?

Before looking at specific prices, it helps to understand what makes tutor costs go up or down. Several factors determine how much you will pay for a lesson.

Tutor experience and qualifications. Native English speakers with teaching certifications like TEFL or CELTA typically charge more than non-certified tutors. A tutor with five years of experience will usually cost more than someone just starting out.

Lesson length. Most platforms offer 30-minute and 60-minute lessons. Longer sessions usually give you a small discount per minute, but shorter lessons can be easier to fit into a busy schedule.

Platform fees. Every tutoring platform adds its own service fee on top of what tutors charge. Some platforms take 15 percent of the tutor's rate, while others charge flat subscription fees. These fees affect the final price you pay.

Package deals. Buying lessons in bulk often reduces the cost per session. Many platforms offer discounts when you purchase 10, 20, or more lessons at once.

Average prices by platform type

Online English tutoring platforms generally fall into three categories, each with its own pricing structure.

Marketplace platforms

Marketplace platforms like iTalki, Preply, and See Guru host thousands of tutors. Prices vary widely based on tutor experience. Here is what you can expect:

  • Budget tutors: $5 to $15 per hour
  • Mid-range tutors: $15 to $30 per hour
  • Premium tutors: $30 to $60 per hour

See Guru is one of the most affordable options in this category. New tutors on See Guru often start at $10 per session, making it a great choice for beginners who want professional lessons without spending a lot of money.

Subscription platforms

Platforms like Cambly and Verbling work on subscription models. You pay a monthly fee for access to unlimited or set numbers of lessons.

  • Cambly: $99 to $199 per month
  • Verbling: $40 to $100+ per month depending on plan
  • Preply (subscription): $89 to $199 per month

Subscription platforms work well if you plan to take multiple lessons per week. The monthly cost can be higher than pay-as-you-go options, but you often get better value per lesson when you use the service frequently.

Professional language schools

Some learners prefer structured programs from established language schools. These tend to be the most expensive option.

  • EF Education First: $1,000 to $3,000 for courses
  • British Council: $20 to $50 per lesson
  • Wall Street English: $2,000 to $5,000 for programs

These programs often include additional materials, progress tracking, and certificates. However, the high prices make them less popular for casual learners or those on tight budgets.

Breaking down the real costs: what you get for your money

Understanding what is included in your lesson price helps you decide if a platform offers good value. Here is what different price ranges typically include.

Under $15 per hour

$15 to $30 per hour

  • Experienced native speakers

$30+ per hour

  • Highly experienced or specialized tutors
  • Business or exam preparation expertise

Hidden fees and extra costs to watch for

When comparing platforms, look beyond the base lesson price. Some costs are not obvious at first glance.

Platform service fees. Some platforms charge a percentage on top of tutor rates. If a tutor charges $20 per hour and the platform takes 20 percent, you pay $24. Always check what the total cost will be.

Currency conversion fees. If you are paying in a different currency, your bank or payment processor may charge conversion fees. These small amounts add up over time.

Package expiration. Some platforms set expiration dates on lesson packages. If you buy 20 lessons but only use 15 before the deadline, you lose the remaining five.

Setup or subscription fees. A few platforms charge monthly membership fees separate from your lesson costs. Make sure you understand all recurring charges before signing up.

How to find affordable English tutors without sacrificing quality

You do not need to spend a lot of money to improve your English. Here are practical tips for getting good lessons at lower prices.

Start with newer tutors. Many talented tutors are just building their student base. They often charge lower prices while providing excellent lessons. As you find tutors you like, you can stick with them as their prices increase.

Book trial lessons. Most platforms offer discounted or free trial lessons. Use these to find a tutor whose teaching style fits your needs before committing to regular sessions.

Buy in bulk during sales. Many platforms run holiday sales or first-time buyer discounts. Stock up on lesson packages during these promotions to save money.

Mix and match. Combine cheap practice sessions with occasional premium lessons. Use budget tutors for regular conversation practice, and save premium tutors for specific skills like business English or exam prep.
