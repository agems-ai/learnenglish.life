Why 1-on-1 Tutoring Makes a Difference

Group classes and apps can help you practice, but they cannot adapt to your specific needs. In a group class, the teacher moves at the pace of the average student. In a 1-on-1 session, every minute is focused on you.

A good 1-on-1 tutor identifies your weak points, corrects your mistakes in real time, and builds a lesson plan around your goals. Whether you are preparing for a job interview, an exam, or just want to speak more confidently, personal attention accelerates your progress in ways group learning rarely can.

The good news is that 1-on-1 tutoring is no longer only for people with large budgets. Several platforms now offer quality personal lessons for under $15 per session.

iTalki: Most Tutor Options Under $15

iTalki has the largest selection of tutors under $15. Community tutors, who are fluent speakers without formal teaching certifications, typically charge between $4 and $10 per lesson. Many offer excellent conversational practice at this price range.

iTalki also has professional tutors with formal qualifications starting around $10 to $15 per lesson. If you want certified teachers at the $15 ceiling, you will find them here. The platform allows detailed filtering by price, language, specialty, and availability, making it easy to find a good match within your budget.

All iTalki lessons are 1-on-1 video sessions. You can book trial lessons with most tutors to check the fit before committing to regular sessions.

Preply: Best for Structured 1-on-1 Learning

Preply starts at around $10 per lesson for most tutors, putting it at the upper end of the $15 budget. What you get for the slightly higher price includes a structured curriculum, lesson recording, a built-in vocabulary trainer, and conversation topics organized by level.

Preply has over 50,000 tutors, so you have more choice than almost any other platform. Free trial lessons let you test a tutor before buying a package. For learners who want a clear study path and access to experienced tutors, Preply at $10 to $15 per lesson is worth the investment.

Cambly: 1-on-1 Conversations on a Budget

Cambly operates on a subscription model. Unlimited conversations start at $99 per month, which works out to roughly $3 to $4 per session if you use it regularly. All tutors on Cambly are native English speakers.

If you plan to take three or more lessons per week, Cambly can be excellent value. The 1-on-1 sessions are real conversations, and you can book tutors who specialize in areas like business English or exam prep. However, Cambly is less structured than platforms with formal curricula. If you want a clear lesson plan, Preply or iTalki professional tutors are better suited.

Verbling: Best for Targeted Language Coaching

Verbling starts at $15 per lesson, sitting right at the edge of this comparison range. The platform has a strong reputation for connecting learners with experienced language coaches who offer personalized, conversation-driven sessions.

Verbling is best for learners who want to focus on specific areas like accent reduction, business vocabulary, or cultural fluency. Tutors on the platform tend to be more experienced, and the pay-per-lesson model means you only pay for what you book.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are tutors under $15 per lesson actually good?

Yes. Many excellent tutors charge moderate rates. Community tutors on iTalki, for example, charge $4 to $10 per lesson and often provide high-quality conversational practice. Price alone is not a reliable quality indicator. Look at tutor reviews, response rates, and intro videos before booking.

Can I get a native English speaker for under $15?

Sometimes. Platforms like iTalki and Preply have native English speakers at various price points. On iTalki, you may find community tutors who are native speakers at $8 to $12 per lesson. On Cambly, native-only tutors are available through subscription. See Guru has a limited selection of native speakers.

Do I need to subscribe or buy a package?

Not on See Guru, iTalki, or Verbling. Those platforms let you book and pay for individual lessons. Cambly and Lingoda require subscriptions. If you prefer pay-per-lesson flexibility, stick with the former three.

How do I find the best tutor for my goals under $15?

Use filters. On iTalki, set your price range, select your target language, and filter by specialty (business, exam prep, conversation). Read at least three reviews per tutor before booking. Book a trial lesson first whenever possible.

What is the best platform for exam prep under $15?

iTalki is our top pick for exam prep under $15. Look for community tutors with positive reviews mentioning IELTS, TOEFL, or your target exam. Many have specific experience with these tests. See Guru also has tutors who specialize in exam preparation.
