Duolingo vs Babbel 2026: Which English Learning App Wins?

↓ Jump to Verdict

Two of the most popular English learning apps go head to head. We compare pricing, learning style, content depth, and speaking practice to help you choose.

Quick Verdict

Choose Duolingo if you want to learn for free, enjoy game-like lessons, and prefer short daily sessions on your phone. Choose Babbel if you want a structured curriculum, are serious about grammar, and want lessons that teach you to hold real conversations from day one.

Our pick for most learners: Duolingo for beginners on a budget. Babbel for intermediate learners who want deeper grammar and conversation skills.

Pricing

Duolingo

Duolingo operates on a freemium model. The free version gives you access to the full tree of lessons, daily reviews, streaks, and most exercises. You can make real progress without spending a cent.

Duolingo Super removes ads and unlocks unlimited hearts, offline mode, and full access to all stories and listening exercises. Super costs $12.99 per month, or around $6.99 per month on an annual plan.

Babbel

Babbel does not offer a meaningful free version. You can try the first lesson of any course at no cost, but everything else requires a paid subscription. Prices range from $13.95/month to $6.95/month on an annual plan.

Learning Approach

Duolingo: Gamified and Addictive

Duolingo turns learning into a game. Every lesson you complete earns you experience points. Miss a day and you lose your streak. The streak system motivates daily practice and keeps users coming back.

Babbel: Structured and Practical

Babbel takes a more traditional approach. Each lesson is built around a specific conversation scenario. Vocabulary and grammar are taught together, with clear explanations of how sentences are formed.
