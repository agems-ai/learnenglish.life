What Are the Main Differences?

Busuu is a structured language learning platform. It provides a complete curriculum, vocabulary, grammar, reading, listening, and speaking, mapped to CEFR levels from A1 to B2. You work through units at your own pace, get AI feedback on writing and pronunciation, and can earn an official certification recognised by McGraw-Hill Education.

iTalki is a tutoring marketplace. There is no built-in curriculum, instead, you browse thousands of human tutors, read their profiles, and book live video lessons when it suits you. The learning is entirely conversation-based: you talk, the tutor gives feedback, you talk some more. The pace and content depend on your tutor and your goals.

The fundamental difference is self-study vs live tutoring. Busuu gives you a map and lets you walk it yourself. iTalki puts you in a room with a guide who adapts to your every question and mistake in real time. Both approaches work, the right choice depends on how you learn best and what you are trying to achieve.

Detailed Analysis

Learning Structure

Busuu: A Clear Path From Beginner to Advanced

Busuu's greatest strength is its structure. The curriculum is mapped to CEFR levels, so you always know exactly where you are and what comes next. Units cover vocabulary, grammar, dialogues, and speaking practice, all designed to build on each other in a logical progression. This makes it easy to study consistently without having to decide what to do next.

The community correction feature is also valuable: native speakers review your written exercises and give feedback. This adds a human element to the self-study experience, making it feel less isolated than some other apps.

iTalki: Flexible, Human-Led Learning

iTalki's learning is as structured, or as unstructured, as you make it. If you want a structured path, you work with a tutor who designs a lesson plan for you. If you prefer free conversation, you just talk. Some learners thrive in this flexibility; others need the guardrails that Busuu provides.

The key advantage is human adaptability. Your tutor hears you speak, notices patterns in your mistakes, and explains things in context, something AI cannot replicate. They can answer your specific questions, push you when you are coasting, and slow down when you are struggling. This real-time responsiveness is iTalki's biggest selling point.

Speaking Practice

Busuu offers AI-powered speaking practice with speech recognition that gives feedback on your pronunciation. This is genuinely useful for drilling specific sounds and phrases. However, it cannot hold a conversation or respond to what you say in real time.

iTalki's speaking practice is conversation with a human being. You talk, they respond, you talk back. This is the fastest way to build real conversational ability because every mistake is caught immediately and every question is answered naturally.

For the best results, use both: Busuu to build vocabulary and study grammar through its structured exercises, then iTalki to put everything into practice in live conversations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Busuu or iTalki better for learning English?

It depends on your goals and learning style. Busuu is better if you want structured, self-paced learning with a clear curriculum and AI tools for writing and pronunciation practice. iTalki is better if you want to develop real speaking ability through live conversations with human tutors. Many learners use both, Busuu for grammar and vocabulary, iTalki for speaking practice.

Which is cheaper, Busuu or iTalki?

Busuu costs from $13.99/month for full access to all courses and features. iTalki lessons start at $5 with community tutors, but you pay per lesson. If you only need a few lessons per month, iTalki could be cheaper. If you plan to practice daily with structured content, Busuu's subscription may offer better value.

Can I use both Busuu and iTalki together?

Yes, this is actually one of the most effective combinations. Use Busuu to build vocabulary, grammar, and reading comprehension through its structured curriculum. Book iTalki sessions weekly to practice what you have learned in real conversations. This gives you the best of both worlds: a clear learning path plus human speaking practice.

Does Busuu have speaking practice?

Busuu has AI-powered speaking practice through its speech recognition feature, which gives feedback on pronunciation. However, this is not the same as live conversation. You can practice individual words and phrases, but you cannot hold a spontaneous conversation with the AI. For real conversational ability, iTalki's live human tutors are significantly more effective.

Which platform is better for beginners?

Busuu is generally better for beginners who want a structured, guided path through the fundamentals. Its curriculum spans CEFR A1 to B2 with clear milestones and exercises. iTalki is better for beginners who are comfortable jumping into conversation and want immediate speaking practice from day one, though the lack of structured content means you may not build as solid a foundation in grammar and vocabulary.
