Cambly Overview

Cambly positions itself as a conversation practice platform rather than a structured course. For $9.99/month (annual plan), you get unlimited access to native English speakers for 1-on-1 video chats. There is no curriculum, no homework, and no progress tracking. You book a tutor, join a call, and talk.

The platform connects you with tutors from the US, UK, Australia, and Canada. Sessions range from 15 to 60 minutes and you can join from desktop or mobile. Cambly also offers group sessions called "Cabals" where multiple learners practice together.

Cambly Pros

  • Unlimited conversation practice at one flat monthly rate
  • Access to native speakers from US, UK, Canada, Australia
  • Flexible scheduling with on-demand tutors available 24/7
  • Mobile app for learning on the go
  • Group "Cabals" sessions included in subscription

Cambly Cons

  • No structured curriculum or lesson plans
  • No way to track progress or set learning goals
  • Tutor quality varies significantly between sessions
  • Some tutors cancel or reschedule frequently

Lingoda Overview

Lingoda takes the opposite approach. This Berlin-based platform offers structured group and private English classes taught by qualified, non-native but fluent teachers. Classes follow a curriculum aligned to the CEFR framework, and you earn certificates upon completion.

Sessions cost around $8 per class for group sessions and $15 for private lessons. You can buy class packs or subscribe monthly. Lingoda's teachers are based in Europe, so time zones may not suit learners in Asia or the Americas as well.

Lingoda Pros

  • Structured curriculum aligned to CEFR levels (A1-C2)
  • Official certification upon completion
  • Small group classes (max 6 students) for focused learning
  • Private lesson option for personalized attention
  • Course materials and homework included

Lingoda Cons

  • Higher cost per session compared to Cambly
  • Non-native teachers (though fluent and qualified)
  • Fixed class schedules require planning ahead
  • Time zones may not suit all regions

Head-to-Head: Cambly vs Lingoda

The core difference is structure versus flexibility. Cambly is ideal if you want to practice speaking without a plan. Lingoda is better if you want to follow a course and measure progress.

Speaking Practice

Cambly wins on volume. With unlimited sessions at $9.99/month, you can practice every day without watching costs. Lingoda charges per session, so each conversation has a price tag. If your goal is maximum speaking time at minimum cost, Cambly takes this round.

Curriculum Quality

Lingoda wins here. Its CEFR-aligned curriculum gives learners a clear path from A1 to C2. Cambly has no curriculum at all. For structured progression and recognized certification, Lingoda is the clear choice.

Teacher Quality

Both platforms employ qualified teachers. Cambly's tutors are native English speakers from US/UK/AU/CA. Lingoda's teachers are fluent non-native speakers with teaching qualifications. Native accent exposure gives Cambly an edge for pronunciation, but Lingoda's teachers may be more structured in their approach.

Price

Cambly is significantly cheaper for heavy users. At $9.99/month for unlimited talking, you could have 30+ sessions for the cost of 4 Lingoda classes. However, Lingoda's per-session model lets you learn less intensively without paying for a monthly subscription you will not use.

Who Should Choose Cambly

  • Learners who want maximum conversation practice on a tight budget
  • People who prefer flexible, on-demand scheduling
  • Intermediate to advanced speakers wanting to maintain fluency
  • Users who prioritize native speaker exposure over curriculum

Who Should Choose Lingoda

  • Beginners who need structured lesson plans and clear progression
  • Learners seeking official certification for job or study applications
  • People who prefer small group classes over 1-on-1 sessions
  • Learners comfortable with European time zones

FAQ

Is Cambly cheaper than Lingoda?

Yes. Cambly's unlimited plan costs $9.99/month, while Lingoda charges $8 per session. For heavy learners, Cambly offers better value. For casual learners, Lingoda's per-session model may be more cost-effective.

Does Cambly have a structured curriculum?

No. Cambly is a conversation practice platform. There are no lesson plans, homework assignments, or progress tracking. You join a call and talk with your tutor. If you need structure, look at Lingoda or See Guru.

Can I earn a certificate with Lingoda?

Yes. Lingoda issues certificates upon completing its curriculum levels. These align with CEFR standards (A1-C2) and may be recognized by employers and educational institutions.

Are Lingoda teachers native English speakers?

Lingoda uses qualified non-native English speakers as teachers. They are fluent, trained, and certified. However, if native accent exposure is important to you, Cambly and See Guru offer more access to native speakers.

Which platform is better for beginners?

Lingoda is better for beginners who need structured lessons and clear progression. Cambly's free-form conversations can be overwhelming for new learners. See Guru also offers structured beginner courses alongside conversation practice.
